# Birth Year Age and 90-Day Date Guide

This repository explains simple age and date calculations, including how to estimate age from a birth year, when a birthday changes the answer, and how to understand 90-day date ranges.

It is useful for quick questions like:

- Born in 1971 and the year is 1989, how old is the person?
- How old are you if you were born in 1994?
- Born in 1996, how old are you?
- How long is 90 days?
- What was 90 days ago?

## Quick Age Rule

To estimate age from a birth year:

```text
Target year - birth year
But this only gives the age after the birthday has passed in the target year.

If the birthday has not happened yet, subtract one more year.

Why Birth Year Alone Is Not Exact

A birth year gives a quick estimate, but the exact age depends on the full date of birth.

For example, two people born in 1994 can have different ages on the same date. One birthday may have already passed, while the other may still be coming later in the year.

90-Day Date Calculations

90 days means 90 actual calendar days.

It is close to three months, but it is not always exactly three calendar months because months have different lengths.

For accurate date calculations, count 90 calendar days forward or backward instead of assuming it means three months.
